Project Overview

Research Question: How has time allocation changed in Peru between 2010 and 2024, and how do these patterns compare with the United Kingdom in 2024?

Motivation: Understanding time use patterns reveals important insights about gender equality, work-life balance, and cultural differences across countries and time periods.

Data Source: National time use surveys (ENUT-Peru, ATUS-UK)

Key Findings

1. Gender Inequality Persists

Across all countries and years, women allocate significantly more time to unpaid care work and household activities than men. In Peru 2010, women spent ~240 minutes daily on these activities compared to ~95 minutes for men.

2. Work-Life Balance Challenges

In both Peru and the UK, individuals combining paid work with household/care responsibilities show limited leisure time. This is especially pronounced for women.

3. Cross-Country Differences

Peru shows longer commute times compared to the UK, likely reflecting geographic and infrastructure differences. Leisure time patterns also differ, suggesting different cultural approaches to free time.

4. Limited Change in Peru (2010-2024)

Despite economic growth and policy interventions, gender gaps in care work have not significantly narrowed in Peru over the 14-year period, pointing to structural challenges.

Policy Implications

  • Childcare and elder care infrastructure expansion needed
  • Flexible work arrangements to enable work-life balance
  • Gender-responsive labor policies and social protection programs
  • Public campaigns to shift attitudes toward shared household responsibilities

Methodology & Data Notes

Data Sources

  • Peru 2010: Encuesta Nacional de Uso del Tiempo (ENUT)
  • Peru 2024: ENUT 2024 round (or equivalent survey)
  • UK 2024: American Time Use Survey (ATUS) data adapted / UK Time Use Survey

Visualization Approach

All charts were created using Vega-Lite, a declarative visualization grammar built on D3.js. This allows for interactive exploration of data patterns while maintaining publication-quality aesthetics.

Limitations

  • Data is based on survey samples and may not capture all population diversity
  • Recall bias in self-reported time use
  • Data collection methods may differ across countries
  • Single-day diaries may not represent typical patterns
