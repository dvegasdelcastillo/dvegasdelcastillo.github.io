PP434: Use of Time Analysis

Academic

Overview: Comprehensive data science portfolio analyzing time allocation patterns in Peru (2010, 2024) and the United Kingdom (2024), exploring gender inequality, work-life balance, and cross-country differences.

Key Features:

  • 4 interactive Vega-Lite visualizations with drill-down functionality
  • Time series analysis of 14-year trend in Peru
  • Gender gap analysis across care work and household activities
  • Cross-country comparative analysis (Peru vs. UK)
  • Policy implications and recommendations

Key Findings:

  • Gender gaps in unpaid care work persist across all countries studied
  • Limited progress on gender equality in Peru despite 14 years of economic growth
  • Policy implications for childcare infrastructure and flexible work arrangements
  • Work-life balance challenges disproportionately affect women

Technologies: Vega-Lite, D3.js, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Data Analysis

Data Sources: National time use surveys (ENUT-Peru, ATUS-UK)
